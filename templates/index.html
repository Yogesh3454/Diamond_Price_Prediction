Understanding Diamond Parameters

This guide provides definitions and explanations for the parameters used in the Diamond Price Prediction model.

Carat

The carat is the unit of measurement for the weight of a diamond. One carat is equivalent to 0.2 grams.

Depth

The depth percentage is the height of the diamond (from the table to the culet) divided by its average diameter. It affects the diamond's brilliance.

Table

The table is the largest facet on the diamond's top. The table percentage is the width of the table divided by the total width of the diamond.

X, Y, Z

  • X: The length of the diamond in millimeters.
  • Y: The width of the diamond in millimeters.
  • Z: The depth of the diamond in millimeters.

Cut

The cut grade refers to how well the diamond has been cut and shaped. It impacts how the diamond reflects light and includes the following grades:

  • Fair: Reflects less light and is less brilliant.
  • Good: Reflects most light but not as much as higher grades.
  • Very Good: Reflects nearly all the light that enters.
  • Premium: Superior quality, reflects almost as much light as the ideal cut.
  • Ideal: Best cut grade, reflects nearly all the light that enters.

Color

Color refers to the absence of color in a diamond. Grades range from D (colorless) to J (lightly colored). The less color, the higher the value:

  • D: Completely colorless, rarest and most valuable.
  • E: Nearly colorless, minute traces of color detectable only by experts.
  • F: Slight color, still considered high quality.
  • G-H: Nearly colorless, good value.
  • I-J: Noticeable color but still appealing, more affordable.

Clarity

Clarity measures the internal and external imperfections of the diamond. The grades include:

  • I1: Included, visible inclusions.
  • SI2: Slightly included, visible under 10x magnification.
  • SI1: Slightly included, difficult to see with the naked eye.
  • VS2: Very slightly included, minor inclusions visible under magnification.
  • VS1: Very slightly included, even fewer inclusions.
  • VVS2: Very, very slightly included, minute inclusions.
  • VVS1: Higher quality than VVS2, nearly flawless.
  • IF: Internally flawless, no inclusions visible.
